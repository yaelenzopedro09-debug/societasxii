Bringing "Apo on the Wall" to life through dramatic interpretation and historical reflection

Our Dramatization

Performance Task

Our group's performance of "Apo on the Wall" was designed to bring the poem's words to life through movement, expression, and staging. Each actor took on a specific role that embodied a moment in the poem, with actions carefully matched to the lines they delivered.

From pointing to the imaginary photo of "Apo" on the wall, to the soldier-like posture of the father, and the fearful glances of the child, our acting aimed to capture both the literal and emotional details of the text. Props such as a picture frame and symbolic gestures helped the audience visualize the constant presence of authority.

"The sequence built toward the climax, where 'Apo's' watchful gaze became overwhelming, ending with the child retreating in fear, a powerful physical representation of the tension the poem conveys."

Historical Context

Martial Law Realities

Our dramatization aims to bring to life the reality of what it was like to live under Martial Law, a time when power and control were not just political concepts but constant, everyday realities. We portrayed how this presence could be felt in even the smallest moments of daily life.

The characters' movements, from their stiff, almost mechanical obedience to their silent expressions of unease, reflected the discipline, surveillance, and fear that ordinary people carried with them. By keeping "Apo" in the background as a silent yet dominating presence, we wanted to show how authority during that era was inescapable, always there, always watching, even in the most private spaces like the home.

Impact on Children

Childhood Under Watch

We also made sure to show how this atmosphere affected children, who were not spared from the tension. The child in the scene is surrounded by symbols of power, uniforms, medals, and weapons — things that should have been far from a child's world.

This mirrors the way history tells us that even the youngest members of society lived under the shadow of authority, growing up with a constant awareness that someone was watching.

"Through our performance, we wanted the audience to not only see but feel what it meant to live under such conditions."

Message & Reflection

Remembering History

Through our performance, we wanted the audience to not only see but feel what it meant to live under such conditions. It's a reminder that the freedoms we enjoy today were not always here, and that they must be valued and protected.

Most of all, it is a call to remember our history so that power, if it must watch over the people, does so to protect them, never to oppress.

Our performance serves as both an artistic interpretation and historical reflection, connecting the poetic narrative with the harsh realities of Martial Law era Philippines.
